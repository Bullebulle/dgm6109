Introduction

The main focus of my research is to explore the factors that influence toilet duration. I began collecting data on October 5th and continued until November 3rd, for a total of 18 days and 126 records. The dataset includes both objective and subjective datas.

Objective Data:

Date

Toilet duration (minutes)

Time spent on my phone during each toilet visit(minutes)

Daily number of toilet visits

Daily amount of water/beverage intake (ml),

Daily amount of dietary fiber intake (g)

Phone distraction level(1-10 point)

Phone content category (game, news, social media)

Subjective Data

Degree of phone distraction during each toilet visit (1-10 points)

motion score before each toilet visit (1-10)

After creating and analyzing multiple scatterplots during previous exercises, I decided to focus on the relationships among five key variables:

Toilet Duration

Phone Content Category

Phone Distraction Level

Emotion score

Phone Use Duration(time spent on the phone during each toilet visit)

Through several rounds of hypothesis testing, I refined my research focus and selected three hypotheses to visualize and evaluate in this project:

To test and visualize these hypotheses, I developed three different types of visualizations:

A scatterplot for Hypothesis 1,

An area trend chart for Hypothesis 2,

A cluster bubble map for Hypothesis 3.

Each visualization emphasizes a distinct relationship among my tracked variables and contributes to understanding how phone usage behavior affects toilet duration.

Sketch 1 Scatterplot: Relationship Between Phone Use Time and Toilet Hour

Hypothesis: As my phone use distraction level increases, my time spent on the toilet increases. In addition, phone content category might positively affect my time spent on the toilet.

Each bubble represents one toilet event. The X-axis shows time spent on my phone during each toilet visit (minutes), and the Y-axis shows total toilet duration (minutes).

The size of each bubble encodes the degree of phone distraction (1-10), and the color represents phone content type(Game:Oringe; >News: Blue; Social Media: Pink; None((do not use the phone)): Grey).

From this figure, a clear upward trend can be observed: data points cluster along a diagonal rising from the lower left to the upper right, indicating that the longer the phone is used, the longer the toilet duration tends to be.

Larger bubbles—representing higher levels of distraction—appear mainly in the upper-right area, further reinforcing this positive correlation. Color patterns reveal that entertainment-related content (such as games and social media) is typically associated with longer toilet durations than informational content (news) or cases without phone use. Together, these visual patterns confirm that both higher distraction levels and specific content types contribute to extended toilet duration.

Conclusion

The visualization supports Hypothesis 1

Sketch 2 Area Chart: Toilet Duration vs Emotion Score by Content Type

Hypothesis: As emotion score increases, toilet duration tends to increase. In addition, phone content type might affect this relationship.

In this area chart, the X-axis represents emotion score(1-10point) and the Y-axis represents toilet duration (0-30minutes). The colored areas correspond to different phone content types(Game:Oringe; >News: Blue; Social Media: Pink; None((do not use the phone)):Grey).

In the area chart, toilet duration gradually rises along the X-axis as emotion score increases.

The stacked colored regions show that game and social-media areas dominate at higher emotion scores, while news or “no phone” areas remain lower. This demonstrates that positive or heightened emotional states correspond to longer toilet durations, especially when the user engages with entertainment content.

Conclusion

The smooth upward slopes and color segmentation together visualize the joint influence of emotional intensity and content type on toilet behavior, supporting the proposed hypothesis.

Sketch 3 Cluster Bubble Map: Toilet Duration vs Emotion Score by Content Type

Hypothesis: As phone distraction increases, toilet duration increases. In addition, entertainment content such as games or social media might reinforce this effect, especially under higher emotion scores.

This cluster bubble map organizes toilet session data across two categorical dimensions: emotion level(1-10point) (columns) and phone distraction level(1-10point) (rows). Each circle represents one toilet event. The size of each bubble corresponds to the toilet duration (minutes), while the color indicates the phone content type(Game:Oringe; >News: Blue; Social Media: Pink; None((do not use the phone)):Grey)

By arranging data in a 3*3 grid, the map visually compares patterns across combinations of emotional and distraction states. Clusters in the lower-right region (high emotion + high distraction) tend to include larger and more numerous bubbles, suggesting that both higher distraction and emotional intensity are associated with longer toilet durations. Within these clusters, entertainment-related content (such as games and social media) appears more frequently, supporting the hypothesis that content type may strengthen the relationship.

In addition, positions within each cell are not numerically meaningful but rather indicate the density and relative frequency of events within that group. This structure emphasizes categorical comparison and pattern recognition instead of continuous correlation.

Conclusion

The pattern reveals that toilet sessions become longer under conditions of high distraction and high emotional arousal, confirming the interactive effect described in the hypothesis. This categorical clustering visually demonstrates how psychological and behavioral variables combine to influence toilet duration.
